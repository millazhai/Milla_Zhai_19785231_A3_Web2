About

Born in Catalonia on the 25th of June, 1982, the Spainish architect was renowend internationally for his
influence on modernism in design. A ground breaker, his design were unique and experimental works of
art, recieiving commision to restructure the affluent homes in Barcelona. His inspiration is drawn upon
nature, having spent many summers in Riudoms, he traced the origins and patterns of mother nature
through a cooperative process. His work is characterised by free form, voluptuous colour and texture,
complex geometries and organic unity. The sea and mountainscapes were his main forms of inspiration.

He dedicated the rest of his life to building the church of the Holy Family in the beginning of 1883. He secluded himself to living at the church while it underwent construction. In 1926, at age of 73, tragedy struck, as the architect was hit by a tram on his way to the Sagrada that evening and passed away from the injuries sustained. His legacy of the Sagrada, wouldn't continue construction until a decade later, where the church had only been 1/4 completed.

Today, it's still under construction and is predicted completion date is 2026.




Legacy

Casa Batlló is a proclamation of joy, a canvas of marine inspiration, a dream world that evokes nature and fantasy.
Casa Vicens. Commissioned by Pere Màrtir Calvet, Calvet was built as a traditional rental apartment building. One of Gaudi's most conservative buildings.

Casa botines. A Modernist building in León. Currently serves as a museum dedicated to Gaudi, Spanish art of the 19th and 20th centuries, and the history of the building itself.

La Sagrada. A one-of-a-kind temple, for its origins, foundation and purpose. Elements of Art Nouveau, Catalan Modernism and Spanish Late Gothic design. The Roman Catholic basilica of La Sagrada Família (The Holy Family) is one of Barcelona's most famous landmarks.


Parc Guell. One of the largest green spaces in Barcelona, sitting on 17 hectares, next to the Sierra de Collserola. With urbanization in mind, Eusebi Güell converted it into a municipal garden.


Casa Mila. Known as La Pedrera or "The stone quarry", a reference to its unconventional rough-hewn appearance. It was the last private residence designed by architect Antoni Gaudí and was built between 1906 and 1912.
